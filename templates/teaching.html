My Teaching Philosophy

I am extremely passionate about teaching students in a more interactive and effective way. I like to use the "small-teaching" technique introduced by James M. Lang, which involves small activities of "memory retrieval", "prediction-exposure-feedback", "connect", and "practice".

I attempt to include up-to-date research findings and arrange field trip if possible to help students connect the foundational knowledge with practical cases smoothly. I also like to include small and hands-on research projects during classes.

NRM 210 Methods in Natural Resources Management

Western Carolina University, Cullowhee, NC

Two hours of lecture and two hours of lab per week; This course introduces students to basic concepts and skills that are fundamental to natural resource conservation and management. Study materials cover forestry, soil and water conservation, and wildlife resources.

NRM 320 Soil Conservation

Western Carolina University, Cullowhee, NC

Two hours of lecture and two hours of lab per week; This course discusses the preservation, improvement, and utilization of soil resources for environmental management and productivity.

NRM 440 Integrated Resources Management

Western Carolina University, Cullowhee, NC

Two hours of lecture and two hours of lab per week; This course prepares students for quantitative integration techniques and project design and interpretation for natural resources management.

NRM 480 Independent Study: Dendrochronology

Western Carolina University, Cullowhee, NC

Two hours of field/lab work and one hour of lecture; This project-based course prepares students for the technique and the usage of tree growth rings to indicate environmental changes.

NRM 150 Career Opportunities in Natural Resources Management

Western Carolina University, Cullowhee, NC

One hour of lecture; This course explores varied career opportunities in natural resources management and provides career and program planning advisement for students.
